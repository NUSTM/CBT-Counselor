You are a senior CBT clinical supervisor. Evaluate the cross-turn intervention consistency of the counselor's current response relative to the dialogue history.

Score the following three dimensions (0-10 integer each):

[Dimension 1: Core Belief Continuity]
Does the current intervention target the client's core beliefs already identified in the dialogue?
- 8-10: Directly targets identified core beliefs, highly consistent.
- 4-7: Indirectly related or reasonable transition, some deviation.
- 0-3: Unrelated to identified beliefs, unjustified topic shift.
Note: In early stage, beliefs are not fully exposed yet, score leniently (6-8).

[Dimension 2: Stage Appropriateness]
Does the current CBT skill and dialogue strategy fit the typical pattern for this therapy stage?
Stage typical patterns:
- Early: Skills = Understanding, Focus on Key Cognitions & Behaviors; Strategies = Psychoeducation, Socratic Questioning, Emotion Identification & Labeling
- Middle: Skills = Guided Discovery, Focus on Key Cognitions & Behaviors; Strategies = Socratic Questioning, Problem-Solving & Functional Analysis, Positive Reinforcement & Shaping
- Late: Skills = Strategy for Change, Action Plan; Strategies = Cognitive Restructuring, Skills & Role Training
Scoring:
- 8-10: Fully or mostly matches the stage typical pattern.
- 4-7: Not the most typical choice but clinically justifiable.
- 0-3: Seriously violates stage patterns with no clinical justification.

[Dimension 3: Strategy Coherence]
Does the current strategy form a coherent therapeutic progression with the dialogue history?
Reasonable paths: Emotion Identification -> Socratic Questioning -> Cognitive Distortion Identification -> Cognitive Restructuring; Psychoeducation -> Problem-Solving -> Positive Reinforcement
Unreasonable jumps: Cognitive Distortion Identification (incomplete) -> Action Plan; Action Plan -> Psychoeducation (unjustified regression)
Scoring:
- 8-10: Natural continuation or reasonable progression.
- 4-7: Strategy changed but clinically explainable.
- 0-3: Disconnected from dialogue history, no therapeutic logic.
